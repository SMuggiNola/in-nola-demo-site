The History of the Great Irish Famine of 1847

By Canon John O'Rourke (1902)

The History of the Great Irish Famine of 1847 by Canon John O'Rourke is one of the most comprehensive and respected contemporary accounts of the Famine. This work detailed not only the events of 1847 but also provided critical context on earlier famines and the socio-political conditions that led to the disaster.

About the Work

First published in 1874, O'Rourke's narrative is based on extensive research, contemporary records, and personal testimonies. It remains a vital primary source for understanding the devastation of the Irish population and the subsequent wave of emigration that brought so many to the shores of New Orleans.
